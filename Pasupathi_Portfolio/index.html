Brand Overview

Warmth, trust, and a touch of celebration

Dream Alliance needed an identity that felt trustworthy enough for a serious life decision, yet warm enough for a service rooted in celebration and tradition. The brief called for a mark and system that would work as comfortably on a wedding-style social post as it would on a business card.

Every decision in the system — colour, type, motif — was built outward from a single signature shape, so the identity could scale across digital and print without losing its character.

Logo Construction & Signature Motif

Two petals, becoming one

The wordmark is anchored by a petal mark built from two overlapping forms — a quiet metaphor for "two becoming one" that carries through the entire system as a recurring device: a section divider, a card accent, even a loading state.

This motif gives the brand a visual signature that's recognisable even when the wordmark isn't present — a deliberate construction choice rather than decoration.

Colour Palette

Drawn from the petal gradient

The palette is pulled directly from the logo mark's plum-to-rose gradient. Plum carries authority, rose and vermilion carry the energy of a wedding invitation, and blush grounds the system as a soft, neutral base.

Typography System

Invitation-card serif, modern sans body

Playfair Display brings the high-contrast, invitation-card character already present in the wordmark, reserved for headlines and emotional moments.

Poppins keeps body copy approachable and modern — a rounded sans that softens the formality of the serif across posters, banners, and the website.

Visual Identity System

One system, every format

The palette, type scale, and petal motif were stress-tested across a business card, a social post template, and a letterhead — proving the system holds up far beyond the logo itself.

This is the layer that makes a brand feel designed rather than decorated: consistent margins, consistent colour logic, consistent voice.
